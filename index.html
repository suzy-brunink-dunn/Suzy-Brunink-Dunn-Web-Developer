portfolio

"Recipe Cards"

These fun recipe cards represent an early static website project I completed through my online courses with Skillcrush. Here I practiced my basic CSS layout skills using and clearing floats, applying the overflow property, and creating images with a static height and width. Since CSS Grid and Flexbox, this is now considered a legacy layout method but one still worth understanding as an important building block.

"Rogue Pickings"

This single page site for a fictional food delivery service was created by pulling all design elements from a PSD file. It then pushed my HTML5 and CSS3 skills up a notch by applying flexbox properies and media queries to make it fully responsive across mobile, tablet and desktop devices.

"Cuppa Joe"

I found the bones of this project through a Codeacademy course on building responsive websites and turned the original project into the fictional coffee shop of my dreams. Here I worked on using responsive CSS and media queries while also managing to give the site a little personality.

"Unplugged"

Unplugged, a fictional tech industry nature retreat, was converted from PSD files to create a mobile first responsive website. This site utilizes an HTML5 Boilerplate shell and features multiple pages, a form for user input, embedded iframe, and responsive typography.

about

My heart: Although I was born and raised in Michigan (the mitten of the United States!) my heart will always belong to N.Y.C. As a kid smitten with theater and film, New York seemed like the capital of the world to me. I moved there many years ago now to attend graduate school at New York University, and although I now live across the river in New Jersey (the armpit of the United States!) the city still inspires me.

My passion: I. Love. Movies. The art of cinema captured my attention as a youngster with perennial favorites such as Grease, The Wizard of Oz, and Willy Wonka & the Chocolate Factory (1971). As I've gotten older, it has continued to hold my attention with personal favorites like Alfred Hitchcock's Rear Window, Julie Dash's Daughters of the Dust, Sally Potter's Orlando, and George Miller's Mad Max: Fury Road to name but a few.

Fun fact: In my college years, I was lucky enough to spend a few weeks in London studying theater (see also: theatre) A definite highlight was seeing Kenneth Branagh perform the titular role in Hamlet. I also spent a semester in California studying filmmaking. Definite highlights there were standing in line behind Martin Kove at a convenience store and spotting Geena Davis in a women's restroom.
